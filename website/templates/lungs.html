How 3D Printing Lungs Works

1. Designing the Lungs

  • First, doctors create a detailed 3D digital model of the patient's lungs. They do this using scans of the patient's body, which helps ensure that the printed lungs will match the patient's exact size and shape.

2. Preparing the "Bioink"

  • Bioink: Instead of regular printer ink, 3D printers use a special substance called bioink. Bioink is made from living cells, often taken from the patient. This ink is carefully mixed with materials that help the cells stick together and grow properly. Because the bioink contains the patient's own cells, it helps reduce the chance of the body rejecting the new lungs.

3. Layer-by-Layer Printing

  • 3D Printing: The 3D printer then starts to print the lungs layer by layer. This process is very detailed because it needs to create not just the shape of the lungs, but also the tiny structures inside them, like the airways and blood vessels. The printer lays down the bioink in a precise pattern, gradually building up the lung tissue.

4. Growing the Lungs

  • Maturation: Once the lungs are printed, they need to mature. This means they are placed in a special environment that mimics the conditions inside the human body. In this environment, the lung cells continue to grow and develop, making the lungs strong and functional.
