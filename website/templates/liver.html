How 3D Printing a Liver Works

1. Designing a Liver

  • First, doctors create a detailed 3D digital model of the patient's liver. They do this using scans of the patient's body, which helps ensure that the printed liver will match the patient's exact size and shape.

2. Preparing the "Bioink"

  • Bioink: Instead of regular printer ink, 3D printers use a special substance called bioink. Bioink is made from living cells, often taken from the patient. This ink is carefully mixed with materials that help the cells stick together and grow properly. Because the bioink contains the patient's own cells, it helps reduce the chance of the body rejecting the new liver.

3. Layer-by-Layer Printing

  • 3D Printing: The 3D printer builds the liver layer by layer. It carefully places the bioink according to the digital model, creating the liver’s complex structure, including its blood vessels and tissues.

4. Maturing the Lungs

  • Maturation: After printing, the liver is placed in a special environment that mimics the conditions inside the human body. This process helps the cells in the liver grow and develop so that the new liver will be ready to function properly once it’s implanted.

5. Transplanting the Lungs

  • Surgery: When the liver is fully matured and has been tested to ensure it works well, surgeons transplant it into the patient’s body. Because the liver is made from the patient’s own cells, it’s more likely to be accepted by their body and work effectively.
