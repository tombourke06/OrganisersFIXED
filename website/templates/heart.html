How 3D Printing a Heart Works

1. Designing the Heart

  • Scanning and Modeling: Doctors would first scan the patient's existing heart or use a computer model to create a 3D digital blueprint of the heart. This model ensures the printed heart matches the patient's exact anatomy.

2. Creating the "Ink"

  • Bioink: Instead of regular ink or plastic, a special material called "bioink" is used. Bioink is made from living cells, often taken from the patient, mixed with a gel-like substance to keep them together. This way, the body is less likely to reject the heart since it's made from the patient’s own cells.

3. Layer-by-Layer Printing

  • 3D Printing: The 3D printer then starts to build the heart layer by layer. This process is very detailed as it forms blood vessels, chambers, and valves.

4. Growing the Heart

  • Maturation: Once the heart is printed, it’s placed in a special environment that mimics the conditions inside the human body, making the heart stronger and ready for transplant.

5. Transplanting the Heart

  • Surgery: When the heart is ready and has been tested, surgeons carefully transplant it into the patient’s body. Because the heart was made with the patient’s cells, it’s more likely to function properly and be accepted by the body.
