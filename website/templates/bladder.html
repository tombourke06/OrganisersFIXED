How 3D Printing a Bladder Works

1. Designing the Blader

  • First, doctors create a 3D digital model of the bladder. This model is based on scans from the patient’s body to ensure that the printed bladder will be the correct size and shape, matching the patient’s anatomy perfectly.

2. Preparing the "Bioink"

  • Bioink: Next, a special material called bioink is prepared. Bioink is made from the patient’s own cells, mixed with a gel-like substance to hold the cells together. Using the patient’s cells helps the body accept the new bladder without rejecting it.

3. Layer-by-Layer Printing

  • 3D Printing: The 3D printer then starts to build the bladder layer by layer. The printer carefully places the bioink in the correct patterns to form the bladder’s walls and other structures. This part is crucial because the bladder needs to be both strong and flexible to store and release urine.

4. Maturing the Bladder

  • Maturation: After printing, the bladder is placed in a special environment that mimics the conditions inside the human body. This step allows the cells in the bladder to grow and strengthen, ensuring it will function like a real bladder once implanted.

5. Transplanting the Bladder

  • Surgery: Once the bladder is fully matured and has passed all necessary tests, surgeons transplant it into the patient’s body. Because the bladder is made from the patient’s own cells, it’s more likely to function properly and be accepted by the body.
